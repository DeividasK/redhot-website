We noticed that our customers often judge the quality of the pellets by their color.
We, being curious creatures, decided to check if this is true.

After learning all the ins and outs of pellet production the old adage trumped again - you can't judge a book by its cover.
And that was also true with ENplus certified pellets.

Even the best pellets may have a darker color tone and it is very hard to ensure that they are always of the same color.

The color of pellets depends on so many factors including the proportion of softwood and hardwood sawdust used, material drying process and even weather temperature during the production.

Furthermore, one may be mislead by uncertified good looking pellets.

These pellets may have weaker mechanical durability, which increases the chances of your stove to be plugged and in need of unplanned expenses.

they may include much more additives in order to make them more durable and whiter, which leads to decreased calorific value.

... a certificate cannot.

If wood pellets have ENplus A1 certification - they are of high quality.

We are so sure of this that we even give you a guarantee - if REDHOT pellets are of poor quality (that is they do not meet ENplus A1 standards) we will refund you 100% of your purchase price.
